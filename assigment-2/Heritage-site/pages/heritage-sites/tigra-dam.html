Tigra Dam

About Tigra Dam

The Tigra Dam, also known as Gwalior Tank, is a prominent dam located around 22 kilometers from Gwalior, India. It is a beautiful and scenic spot that serves as a major water source for the city of Gwalior. The dam is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful getaway for nature lovers and tourists alike.

History

The Tigra Dam was constructed in the 19th century by the Scindia rulers of Gwalior. It was built with the purpose of providing water to the growing city of Gwalior and its surroundings. The dam is located near the Tigra village and is part of the larger water supply network for the city.

Over time, the Tigra Dam has become a popular tourist attraction, thanks to its picturesque setting and the activities available for visitors. It is also home to a variety of local wildlife, making it a wonderful spot for birdwatching and nature walks.
