Suraj Kund

About Suraj Kund

Suraj Kund is an ancient water reservoir located in Gwalior, India. It is an important historical and cultural landmark of the region. The name "Suraj Kund" translates to "The Reservoir of the Sun," and it is believed to have been constructed by the famous ruler, Raja Man Singh, during the 15th century. Suraj Kund is not just an architectural marvel but also a significant site for water conservation in the region.

History

The construction of Suraj Kund is attributed to Raja Man Singh, a prominent ruler of Gwalior. It was built to serve as a water reservoir, providing water to the nearby areas, particularly during dry spells. The structure has a large circular shape and is surrounded by an impressive fortification wall, making it not only a functional water source but also an architectural wonder.

Over the years, Suraj Kund has stood the test of time and remains an important part of Gwalior's heritage. It is also a popular destination for tourists, who visit to admire its historical significance and peaceful surroundings.
