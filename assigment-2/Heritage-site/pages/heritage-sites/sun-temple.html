Sun Temple Gwalior

About Sun Temple Gwalior

The Sun Temple in Gwalior is one of the most famous temples dedicated to the Sun God (Surya). Located at the Gwalior Fort, it is known for its stunning architecture and vibrant atmosphere. The temple is a beautiful blend of traditional Indian and modern architectural styles and attracts a large number of pilgrims and tourists alike.

History

The Sun Temple was built in the 19th century by the Scindia family. The temple's design is based on the famous Sun Temple at Konark in Odisha, featuring a stunning structure and intricately carved walls. The temple is constructed in the shape of a chariot with 24 wheels, representing the 24 hours of the day.

The temple stands on a raised platform, with a grand entrance and a magnificent idol of the Sun God at the center. Over time, the temple has become a significant landmark in Gwalior, known for its religious importance as well as its architectural beauty.
