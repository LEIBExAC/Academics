Gujri Mahal

About Gujri Mahal

The Gujri Mahal is an iconic monument located within the Gwalior Fort. Built during the reign of Raja Man Singh Tomar in the 15th century, it is a stunning example of Mughal architecture, featuring delicate carvings and ornate decorations. The mahal is famously associated with the king's love for his queen, Mrignayani, and is a beautiful expression of their love and devotion.

History

Gujri Mahal, named after the queen, Mrignayani (also known as Gujri), was built by Raja Man Singh Tomar. The story behind the palace is that it was constructed as a gesture of love for his queen, who was from a modest background and was unable to live in the palace due to her lower status. This magnificent palace was built for her, providing a grand and beautiful space for her to reside.

The palace was initially designed as a living space for the queen, with intricate carvings and lavish interiors. The architecture reflects the grandeur of the Mughal period, with elaborate arches and elegant columns. The palace is a testament to the love and reverence the king had for his queen, and it remains an important landmark of Gwalior's royal history.
