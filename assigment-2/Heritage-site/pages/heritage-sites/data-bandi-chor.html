Data Bandi Chhod

About Data Bandi Chhod

Data Bandi Chhod is one of the most significant and revered festivals in Gwalior. The festival is celebrated in remembrance of the release of Guru Hargobind Ji, the sixth Sikh Guru, from imprisonment by the Mughal Emperor Jahangir. It is celebrated with much fervor and devotion in Gwalior, where thousands of devotees gather at the Gurdwaras to celebrate the occasion.

History

The name Data Bandi Chhod literally translates to "The Release of the Prisoner". The festival commemorates Guru Hargobind Ji's release from the Gwalior Fort prison, where he had been held captive by Emperor Jahangir. Guru Hargobind Ji had been imprisoned for a long period, and when he was released, he demanded the release of 52 Hindu princes who were also imprisoned by the Mughal ruler. The festival symbolizes freedom and liberation.

The occasion is celebrated annually in Gwalior, where Sikhs gather at the Gurdwaras and other historical sites to remember Guru Hargobind Ji's courage and sacrifice. The festival includes prayers, hymns, and a procession that symbolizes the liberation of Guru Hargobind Ji and the Hindu princes.
