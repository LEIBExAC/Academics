Sas Bahu Temple

About Sas Bahu Temple

The **Sas Bahu Temple** is an architectural marvel located in the Gwalior Fort. It consists of two temples, built in the 11th century, dedicated to the Hindu gods. The temple complex is known for its intricate carvings and unique architectural style, a fusion of North and South Indian styles of temple construction.

History

The Sas Bahu Temple was constructed by King Mahipal, who dedicated the temples to his mother and his wife. The name "Sas Bahu" refers to the relationship between the two temples, symbolizing the mother (Sas) and daughter-in-law (Bahu). These temples are famous for their stunning carvings on the walls and pillars, which depict various scenes from Hindu mythology.

Built in the 11th century, the Sas Bahu Temple has stood the test of time. The intricately carved images on the temple walls include gods, goddesses, and stories from the epic Ramayana and Mahabharata. This temple is also known for its rich sculptural details, making it an important site for both historical and religious reasons.
